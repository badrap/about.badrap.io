--- layout: health title: Cyber Health for Employees styles: - ../../custom.css heading: Stay safe online subheading: Cyber Health Month for the Employees - Week 2 leftbox: - First: heading: Stolen passwords used to attack other services text: > 27% of us use the same or similar password in different services. Criminals buy or steal usernames and passwords to run automated attacks against other services. Make their life harder by using unique passwords in every service. Pay special attention to your email accounts as they can be used to reset the passwords of other services. - Second: heading: Trust relationships get exploited text: > Criminals might get someone to transfer money to their own accounts. These attacks work because they are timed well and look credible. Criminals like to get hold of email and social media accounts and monitor the messaging to study the target and wait for the right opportunity. They also like to send spam and run less tailored attacks against your contacts. Make sure your social media and email account passwords are not used anywhere else. - Third: heading: Personal information used in scams text: > While Internet services and smartphone apps may have legitimate reasons to collect your personal information, problems arise when the organisations don't adequately protect the data they collect. Criminals can take this information and impersonate you while scamming others. Treat personal information like money. Give it away only when necessary. Remove unnecessary personal details such as your birthday and home address from online services. When a smartphone app asks permission for personal information, such as your contacts or location data, deny it whenever you can. rightbox: - Ingress: heading: Protect Your Personal Information text: > Last week you learned that criminals could use stolen information to conduct further attacks.
