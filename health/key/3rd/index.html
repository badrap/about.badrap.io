--- layout: health title: Cyber Health for Employees styles: - ../../custom.css heading: Stay safe online subheading: Cyber Health Month for the Employees - Week 3 leftbox: - First: heading: Criminals take over vulnerable devices text: > Sometimes attacks against popular Internet-services, such as Netflix or different Internet banks get into the general news. The news rarely tell why these attacks are possible. Criminals use Internet-connected devices which have security vulnerabilities to attack others. They take over vulnerable devices and make them to attack others. If you experience a really slow connection to all the Internet services you try, one of your devices might be participating in these attacks. - Second: heading: They also reflect their attacks via home routers text: > Criminals can also reflect and amplify their attacks by misusing services which are open in home routers. They send packets to the router's service with a forged sender address. The router then responds to the forged address. With hundreds of thousands of routers even the strongest services go down. Typical targets are popular Internet-services, but the same method can be used to take down critical services of our society. Complete this week's mission to check if security researchers have reported your router can be used in attacks. - Third: heading: Ransomware text: > Criminals spread malicious programs via email and try to get you to download them from the net. Some programs are nasty as they encrypt the contents in your hard drive and network drives. After encryption is completed, the program asks for money to open the files. You can't rely on anti-virus to protect your as they can fail to detect the latest versions. Good cyberhygiene practices you learned last week help here also. Think before you click. Open attachents only if you are certain they are legitimate. Before clicking a link, hover your mouse on top of it and let the application show you with where the link actually leads. The real destination is typically shown as a tooltip or in the status bar at the bottom of the window. rightbox: - Ingress: heading: Be Smart with Smart Devices text: > So far you have learned why your personal online safety matters and how to protect your personal information online. This week you will learn if the networks you use to connect to Internet can be hacked, or have already been hacked and used to attack others. - Mission: subheading: Your mission this week text: > Your mission this week is to become aware if you connect to the Internet from networks which are vulnerable to hacking or have already been hacked and used to attack others. Log in to the WiFi at your home and check your home IP with badrap.io. Save your home IP with "Save asset" -button so that you will get email warnings if something is found in future. You can also check other places you visit. A guest WiFi at a hotel or a business meeting, your relatives and so on. Save the ones you want to keep guarding with "Save asset" button. img: ip-assets.jpg img-text: Check more IP-addresses. - How: subheading: Make it a habit text: > Just like in the real-world, good habits lay out the foundation for good online health. It is not easy to change habits, but we all have learned to brush our teeth and wash our hands, so it is possible. In the online world, automation picks up our habits over time. For example software updates are mostly automatic these days. Create a habit of visiting badrap.io once per month. Your que is the montly "All good" -email. Check that you have up to date IP-address for your home. Check also if you have created new email addresses to follow. img: all-good.jpg img-text: Your monthly all-good email is your que to visit badrap and check your assets are up to date. missionbox: heading: Your mission for week 2 text: Take these easy steps to qualify for rewards: steps: - Click the button below to give your employer another plus point and to learn for how many IP-addresses badrap.io has received security reports. - Visit badrap.io from your home WiFi.
