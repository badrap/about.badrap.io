--- layout: health title: Cyber Health for Employees styles: - ../../custom.css heading: Stay safe online subheading: Cyber Health Month for the Employees - Week 3 leftbox: - First: heading: Lock down your home router text: > Attacks against popular Internet-services, such as Netflix or different Internet banks sometimes get into the general news. The news rarely explain why these attacks are possible. The devices you connect to the Internet may have security vulnerabilities. Criminals search vulnerable devices and use them to attack others. They either take over the device, or just reflect their attack traffic. Complete this week's mission to check if security researchers have reported your home router can be used in attacks. - Second: heading: Protect your computer text: > Criminals know how to get around antivirus protection. Good cyber hygiene practices are more important than ever. Criminals now make billions by extorting people and companies. They encrypt files and demand money for unlocking them. They record your activities and threaten to release their material. Last week you learned how criminals exploit trust relationships. Be suspicious even if the email attachment came from your colleague. Before clicking links, hover the mouse on top of it and check from the tooltip or the status bar at the bottom where it actually leads. - Third: heading: Be smart about smart devices text: > TV's, light bulbs, security cameras, and many other devices are now smart and online. Manufacturers look for the cheapest means to get their devices online, and that is bad news for the security. When buying smart devices, think what could happen if someone got access to them. Read about their capabilities. Netamo weather station also measures background noise - so it must have a microphone. Can you access your security camera's feed from the Internet? Maybe some day someone else can too. Place the cameras in a way they have minimal impact to your privacy. rightbox: - Ingress: heading: Be Smart with Devices text: > During this month you have learned why your personal online safety matters and how to protect your personal information online. If you need to refresh your memory, all materials are available here. This week you will learn if the networks you use to connect to the Internet can be hacked, or have already been hacked and used to attack others. - Mission: subheading: Your mission this week text: > Your mission this week is to become aware if the networks you use to connect to the Internet are vulnerable to hacking - or have already been hacked and used to attack others. Start with your home. Connect your personal device, such as your smart phone or home computer to your home WiFi network. Visit badrap.io and check the results for your IP-address. IP-address means the Internet address you use to connect to the Internet. Save your IP with "Save asset" -button to get email warnings if something is found in future. Please note that if you use a laptop from work, it can make badrap.io to think you are coming from your work network and show different results. img: ip-assets.jpg img-text: Check more IP-addresses. - How: subheading: Make it a habit text: > Just like in the real-world, good habits lay out the foundation for good online health. It is not easy to change habits, but we all have learned to brush our teeth and wash our hands, so it is possible. In the online world, automation picks up our habits over time. For example software updates are mostly automatic these days. Create a habit of visiting badrap.io once per month. Your que is the montly "All good" -email. Check that you have up to date IP-address for your home. Check also if you have created new email addresses to follow. img: all-good.jpg img-text: Your monthly all-good email is your que to visit badrap.io and check your asset list is up to date. missionbox: heading: Your mission for week 3 text: Take these easy steps to qualify for rewards: steps: - Click the button below to give your employer another plus point and to learn for how many IP-addresses badrap.io has received security reports. - Visit badrap.io from your home WiFi.
